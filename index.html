WoundChron

Final-Year Project · Wet-Lab + Computational + Software

2025 – 2026

A wound-aging estimation platform built on green-synthesized nanoparticles, validated computationally, and shipped as a working web application — the clearest example of the three-discipline bridge in one project.

ZnO Nanoparticle Synthesis Molecular Docking GEO Transcriptomics React.js
  • Green-synthesized ZnO nanoparticles from Ageratum conyzoides extract, characterized via UV-Vis, XRD, and FTIR; validated antimicrobial activity against E. coli and P. aeruginosa
  • Performed molecular docking (quercetin, −8.3 kcal/mol vs. target 3MME) and GEO transcriptomic biomarker analysis (Scanpy) to support the wound-aging model
  • Built and deployed a React.js web app with role-based access (clinician vs. patient), a JavaScript image-analysis pipeline (Gaussian blur, Otsu thresholding, healing-rate estimation), and automated PDF/CSV reporting
  • Originated as a venture concept through the Ignite Bootcamp (Venture Idea Development) program

Nyctan Chews

Solo Project · Innovation & Design Thinking Course

2025

A natural immunity gummy formulated from Coral Jasmine (Nyctanthes arbor-tristis) extract, taken through a full design-thinking cycle from consumer research to a costed, testable product.

Dual Extraction Agar-Agar Gelation Design Thinking SDG Mapping
  • Used dual extraction — cold maceration to preserve bioactives, heat-assisted for natural coloring — with agar-agar gelation and natural excipients (jaggery, honey, lemon)
  • Validated the formulation physicochemically: pH ~4.2, °Brix ~36°, moisture content 18–20%, plus preliminary antimicrobial testing
  • Backed the product with a cost-per-pack analysis and mapped it to four UN Sustainable Development Goals (3, 9, 12, 15)
